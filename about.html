Microsystems Lab

Studying various physical phenomena through MEMS resonator configuration and exploring the possibility of use of MEMS/NEMS for various engineering applications.

Research Highlights

Our Research Focus Areas

  • Theoretical modeling and experimental study of physics such as
    • Energy loss mechanisms
    • Parametric excitation
    • Nonlinearities and phase noise vis-a-vis different material systems and applied external fields such as fluid flow, temperature gradients, magnetic field etc. via Micro and Nano Electromechanical Systems (MEMS/NEMS)
  • MEMS/NEMS wafer-scale packaging
  • Sensor systems using arrays of resonators
  • Phononic Crystals

    • Technologies Developed

      Fabricated, characterized and modeled frequency change and energy losses in several types of resonators such as suspended microchannel resonators (SMRs), piezoelectric-SOI based cantilevers and membranes, double ended tuning forks and neuristors.
      Built transimpedence and voltage amplifer circuits for low to mid RF applications
      Used an extension of GA algorithms for system identifcation and shape optimization for practical MEMS applications
      Developed the technology, fabricated and measured devices with various material systems namely: AlN films for piezoelectric actuation and sensing, PZT on SOIs, Aluminum, Silicon, NdNiO3 and VO2.
      Optimized epi-seal packaging process
